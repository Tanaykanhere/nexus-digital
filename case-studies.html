Phase 1: The Attribution Crisis

When GlobalTech Inc. approached Nexus, they were facing a plateau. Despite spending $50k/month on Google Ads and LinkedIn, they couldn't accurately measure which campaigns were driving "Sales Qualified Leads" (SQLs) versus just "MQLs."

The existing GA4 setup was defaulting 60% of conversions to "Direct" or "Unassigned" channels. This "Dark Social" problem meant the CMO was effectively guessing where to allocate the next quarter's budget. Their Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) was creeping up to $450, which was unsustainable for their LTV (Lifetime Value).

Phase 2: Full-Funnel Tracking Infrastructure

Our first move was not increasing ad spend—it was fixing the measurement. We implemented a **Server-Side Google Tag Manager (sGTM)** environment to bypass ad-blockers and cookie restrictions.

Key Technical Updates:

  • Enhanced Conversions: Implemented hashing of user data to improve Google Ads match rates by 15%.
  • Custom DataLayer: Built a dataLayer push for CRM events to track "Offline Conversions" back to specific click IDs.
  • BigQuery Export: Configured raw data export to analyze user paths beyond the standard GA4 interface.

Phase 3: Intent-Based Scaling

With clean data flowing, we discovered that their LinkedIn Top-of-Funnel ads were actually the primary drivers of Search volume on Google. We shifted budget from "Broad Match" search terms to a "Search Retargeting" strategy.

We focused on "Search Intent" rather than just "Search Volume." By excluding low-intent audiences and focusing spend on users who had previously engaged with their technical whitepapers, we saw an immediate 22% drop in the cost per demo request.
